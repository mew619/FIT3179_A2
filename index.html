Decline of Japanese Youth Population

This study will showcase the current discrepancy between
elder and younger generation of Japan

Japan has the highest proportion of elderly citizens of any country in the world. The country is experiencing a "super-aging" society both in rural and urban areas. According to 2014 estimates, 33.0% of the Japanese population is above the age of 60, 25.9% are aged 65 or above, and 12.5% are aged 75 or above. People aged 65 and older in Japan make up a quarter of its total population, estimated to reach a third by 2050. (Wikepedia, 2020)

This graph is the visualisation of the 5 largest economies in the world. As can be seen in the graph, compared to every other country, in Japan, the number of elderly people is two times the amount of younger generation.

As can be seen by the graphs, as years pass, the money spent on healthcare has increased significantly which has lead to a higher life expectancy. The birth rate has decreased by an outstanding amount while the death rate has increased. This has lead to the younger demographic of Japan being slowly reduced. Eventually, if there is no change in the current situation, there will come a time when it will become a minority.
